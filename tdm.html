Kaleidescopic forest is a site-specific installation developed at the Harvard university graduate school of design for the course “transformable design methods” with chuck hoberman. It is a kinetic facade of six wooden leaves on the interior to the window of a stairwell in gund hall. The leaves float between floors as their attachment to the mullions is hidden. This playful forest is also a haptic device that invites visitors to turn, open, and hyperextend the leaves.
