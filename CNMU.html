The Mac Dowell Artist Collony (New Hampshire) is undergoing expansion and new dwellings with studio space for artist to rent are planned to be built. For this purpose, a new revolutionary and flexible construction material is needed, as the studio spaces are thought to be flexible and change in size depending on the needs of the artist. Taking the cement masonry unit (CMU) as base, a new hybridized block has been designed for this purpose. The temporary nature of the building and the need for adaptability requires a system that uses no fix connection, avoiding mortar to guarantee the connection between the blocks. Using an ostemorphic shape for the block we ensure the interlock and we provide a dry construction system, allowing future readjustments. To improve the insulation values of the block we propose a hybridization process. Including a polymer core, with a hollow core structure with different air cavities, highly improves the thermal performance of the block. Theoretically, the thermal performance (r value) was calculated by the Bejan Mathematical Model and later a prototype of the block was tested physically using hot boxes to verify the performance.
