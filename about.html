apoorv goyal
about / contact
Apoorv is an environmental designer, currently pursuing his Master in Design Studies (Energy and Environments) at the Harvard Graduate School of Design. Previously, he holds a bachelors in Architecture from the School of Planning and Architecture in New Delhi, India. Apoorv's interests lie in the domain of sustainability in architecture and urban design. He is currently researching on the relation of the built and unbuilt in the cities and the limits of densifying (built) the cities while maximizing the extraction of energy from solar insolation. He is also working on developing a parametric tool that will help urban designers and planners provide sustainability design guidelines in the early stages of design.

After graduating from SPA, Apoorv co-founded a research platform for architecture and urban design - arch i platform in Delhi. Among multiple roles he played at arch i, Apoorv was a part of various projects such as Kargyak Learning Center, various cartography projects, exhibitions like Architecture for Humanity and many more. Later, he joined India's leading green building consultants Environmental Design Solutions Pvt. Ltd. as a green building analyst, where he was involved in a variety of works such as facilitation and documentation of prestigious projects for the green building rating systems - LEED and GRIHA, whole building energy and daylight simulations for the buildings, LEED USGBC review, graphical work for publications and software development. Apoorv also worked recently with EDS on proposing changes related to sustainability in the upcoming TOD policy for the city of Delhi.

At GSD, Apoorv has worked as a research assistant with the Zofnass program for sustainable infrastructure where he contributed in the development of the economic assessment tool for the new ISI Envision Rating System. He is a board member of the IBPSA- Boston chapter and an active member of ASHRAE. He heads two student groups at GSD- GSD Green Design team, India GSD, and is also a board member of the Harvard India Student Group.
